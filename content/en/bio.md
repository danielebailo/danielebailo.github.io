Driven by strong technical creativity and the ability to imagine innovative solutions, **Daniele Bailo** has worked for years at the intersection of **Research Data Management** and **Artificial Intelligence**, developing original approaches for integrating, managing and enhancing data in the Earth Sciences.

He is **IT Officer of EPOS-ERIC**, with strategic responsibilities for the evolution of the European digital infrastructure dedicated to integrated access to multidisciplinary data, services and software.

At INGV he also serves as **Head of the Functional Unit “Computational Geophysics – Information Systems – EPOS”**, coordinating institutional and project activities in information systems, digital infrastructures and AI technologies for geosciences.

He is also **coordinator of projects and groups dedicated to artificial intelligence at INGV**, with a particular focus on deep learning for seismology, generative models and LLM/RAG applications for creating intelligent agents in geosciences.

He is **Scientific Coordinator of European projects** in the *ENVRI, EOSC, VREs* ecosystem and holds roles as **Work Package Leader** and **Task Leader** in numerous Horizon Europe and Horizon 2020 consortia. He has led international technical groups and contributed to defining architectural models, semantic standards, metadata and FAIR processes for the Solid Earth Science community.

His scientific activity includes **numerous contributions in international peer-reviewed journals**, as well as technical reports, proceedings and presentations at European conferences, with a growing focus on the use of artificial intelligence in Earth Sciences and on the ethical dimension of AI.

## Professional experience

### Technologist, level III – INGV / EPOS-ERIC (2016 – present)

Level III technologist at the Istituto Nazionale di Geofisica e Vulcanologia (INGV), within the **EPOS Management Office ICT**.
He serves as **EPOS-ERIC IT Officer**, with responsibility for IT strategy, the technical roadmap of the infrastructure and coordination of the international team developing the **EPOS Data Portal**, an open source platform for integrated access to data, services and software for Earth Sciences. The platform is based on microservice architectures, orchestration via Docker/Kubernetes, federated authentication (OAuth2, OpenID Connect, AARC Blueprint), semantic integration and FAIR data management.

Main activities:

- Coordination of the EPOS international IT team and of the integration of data, services and software from multiple scientific communities.
- Design and development of **Virtual Research Environments** to support the entire data lifecycle (production, analysis, modelling, visualisation, interpretation).
- Definition of software architectures and information models for distributed research infrastructures.
- Experimentation and applications of **deep learning** for seismology (e.g. PhaseNet for automatic picking).
- Design and development of **LLM/RAG** pipelines for intelligent agents and decision-support systems in geosciences.
- Organisation and dissemination of scientific and technical activities, including institutional events such as **AI Day @ INGV**.
- Participation in EPOS governance: IT Board, General Assembly, Executive Committee and Board of Officers.

### Research and development in Material Science – CNR (2005 – 2012)

Research and development activity carried out through contracts and grants at the **CNR – Institute of Structure of Matter**.

During this period he completed a **PhD in Material Science (ISCED 6)** at the *La Sapienza* University of Rome.

**Thesis title:**
*Studi in-situ morfologico/strutturali di film polimerici per dispositivi fotovoltaici organici: proprietà bulk, di superficie e di interfaccia ed effetti di invecchiamento*
([*In-situ Structural/Morphological study of polymer-based active materials for Organic Photovoltaic devices: bulk, surface and interface properties and aging effects*](https://zenodo.org/records/46424))).

Research carried out at the **X-ray spectroscopy group of CNR – Rome/Tor Vergata (Institute of Structure of Matter)**.

Main areas and techniques:

- Organic materials for photovoltaic applications (P3HT:PCBM, PPV:PCBM, buffer layer PEDOT).
- Materials for **gas sensing** applications (Ni phthalocyanines, Co porphyrazine).
- **Energy-dispersive X-ray diffraction** (lattice parameters, grain size).
- **X-ray reflectometry** (thickness, surface and interface roughness).
- **AFM microscopy** for studying high-resolution surface morphology.
